## Winter is harder on your vehicle than most drivers realize

Cold temperatures thicken fluids, stress batteries, and make existing wear issues worse. A car that ran fine all summer can start showing problems the first cold snap of the year.

Getting ahead of it with a proper maintenance check before winter is far less stressful than dealing with a breakdown in January. Here's what actually matters for GTA driving conditions.

## What to check before winter hits

- Battery health, since cold weather is when a weak battery finally fails
- Tire tread depth and air pressure, which drops as temperatures fall
- Coolant concentration, to make sure it's rated for our winter lows
- Wiper blades and washer fluid rated for freezing temperatures
- Brake condition, since winter driving means more sudden stops
- Oil viscosity, if your vehicle benefits from a winter-weight oil

Skipping any one of these on its own usually isn't a problem. They tend to compound, and a weak battery paired with worn tires in the first snowfall is a bad combination.
