## Suspension wear is gradual, which is why it gets ignored

Suspension components wear slowly, so most drivers get used to the change without noticing it happening. By the time it's obvious, the parts have usually been worn for a while.

Catching it earlier means a smaller repair and better handling in the meantime. Here's what to actually pay attention to.

## Common signs your suspension needs a look

- Excessive bouncing after hitting a bump, instead of settling quickly
- Noticeable body roll or leaning through turns
- Clunking or knocking noises over bumps or potholes
- Uneven tire wear, especially on the inside or outside edges
- The car pulling to one side or feeling less stable at highway speed
- Visible fluid leaking from a shock or strut

Any one of these on its own might be minor. Several at once usually means something specific has worn out and it's affecting the rest of the system.
