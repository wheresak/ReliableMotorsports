## Modifying a car you drive every day is a different calculus

A build that only comes out on weekends can prioritize aggressive setups without worrying much about daily comfort or reliability. A daily driver has to handle rush hour, potholes, and cold mornings on top of whatever upgrades you add.

That doesn't mean you shouldn't modify a daily driver. It means the choices matter more.

## What to think through before you start

- How the modification affects ride comfort and reliability during normal commuting
- Whether the parts you're considering are built for daily use or track-focused use
- How the change affects factory warranty coverage, if your vehicle is still under one
- Fitment and finish, since a poorly installed part causes more daily annoyance than a weekend car would ever reveal
- Whether you're modifying for looks, performance, or both, since that changes what's actually worth prioritizing

Bolt-on parts that look great on a build sheet don't always translate to a comfortable daily experience. It's worth being honest about how the car is actually used before committing.
