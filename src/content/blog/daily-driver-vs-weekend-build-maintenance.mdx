## Mileage isn't the only thing that changes your maintenance needs

A daily driver and a weekend build put very different demands on a vehicle, even if they're mechanically similar. How often you drive, how far, and under what conditions all affect what maintenance actually matters most.

Treating both cars the same way usually means over-servicing one and under-servicing the other.

## What each one actually needs

For a daily driver:

- Regular oil changes based on actual mileage, since daily use adds up fast
- Brake and tire wear checks more frequently, given the higher mileage
- Battery and charging system checks, since daily short trips are hard on batteries

For a weekend build or low-mileage car:

- Attention to fluids that degrade with age, not just mileage, including brake fluid and coolant
- Tire condition checks for dry rot and cracking, since low-mileage tires still age
- A pre-drive check before it comes out of storage or sits for weeks, covering battery charge and fluid levels

A low-mileage car sitting for long stretches can develop its own issues, like flat-spotted tires or a slowly draining battery, that a high-mileage daily driver wouldn't see.
