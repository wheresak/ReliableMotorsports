## Why the interval on your dashboard isn't the full picture

Every vehicle ships with a manufacturer-recommended oil change interval, usually somewhere between 8,000 and 16,000 kilometres. That number assumes ideal driving conditions: steady highway speeds, moderate temperatures, and no heavy idling.

Scarborough driving doesn't look like that. Stop-and-go traffic, short trips that never let the engine fully warm up, and cold winters all put more stress on your oil than the manufacturer's test conditions account for. Going strictly by the number on the sticker can mean pushing your oil change frequency further than it should go.

## Signs it's time for an oil change, regardless of mileage

- The oil change reminder light or a mileage interval you've already passed
- Oil that looks dark, gritty, or noticeably low on the dipstick
- A noticeable engine noise or rougher idle than usual
- It's been longer than a year since your last change, even with low mileage
- You mostly drive short trips under 20 minutes
