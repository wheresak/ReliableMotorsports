## A used car can look fine and still hide expensive problems

A clean exterior and a smooth test drive don't tell you much about what's actually going on mechanically. Issues like worn suspension components, early signs of a failing sensor, or a poorly repaired accident can all be invisible during a quick look or short drive.

A pre-purchase inspection exists specifically to catch what a walkaround can't.

## What a proper pre-purchase inspection covers

- A diagnostic scan for stored fault codes, even ones not currently triggering a warning light
- A check of brake, suspension, and steering components for wear
- Fluid condition checks across the engine, transmission, and coolant system
- Signs of prior accident repair or bodywork that wasn't disclosed
- An assessment of anything that looks like it will need attention soon after purchase

The goal isn't to talk you out of a purchase. It's to make sure you know what you're actually buying, including anything that should factor into your decision or the price you're willing to pay.
