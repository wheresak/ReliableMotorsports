## Two different ways to lower your car

Lowering springs and coilovers both drop your ride height and improve handling, but they solve the problem differently. Lowering springs replace your factory springs and work with your existing shocks. Coilovers replace the entire strut and spring assembly with an adjustable unit.

Which one makes sense depends on what you're actually trying to get out of the car, not just how low you want it to sit.

## How to think about the choice

- Lowering springs are the simpler, lower-cost option if your factory shocks are in good shape and you want a modest drop with better handling
- Coilovers let you adjust ride height and often damping, which matters if you're chasing a specific stance or track use
- Lowering springs without matched shocks can wear out your suspension faster than expected
- Coilovers are a bigger investment but give you more control over the final setup
- Your daily use matters, since a daily driver on rough roads has different needs than a car built mainly for shows or track days

There isn't a single right answer here. A build focused on daily comfort with a mild drop usually points toward springs. A build focused on adjustability and a more aggressive setup usually points toward coilovers.
